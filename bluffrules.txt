Bluff (also known as Cheat / I Doubt It / BS)
Objective

Be the first player to get rid of all your cards by either telling the truth or successfully bluffing about the cards you play.

Players
3–8 players (best with 4–6)
1 standard 52-card deck
For 6 or more players, use 2 decks.
Setup
Shuffle the deck.
Deal all cards as evenly as possible.
Players may arrange their cards in any order.
Choose a starting player randomly or let the player with the Ace of Spades start.
Turn Order

Play proceeds clockwise.

The rank being played follows this cycle:

Ace → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 6 → 7 → 8 → 9 → 10 → Jack → Queen → King → Ace → ...

How a Turn Works

On your turn:

Place 1 to 4 cards face down into the center pile.
Announce what you are playing.

Example:

"Two 5s"

You may:

Tell the truth.
Lie completely.
Play some correct cards and some incorrect cards.

No one can look at the cards unless a bluff is called.

Calling Bluff

Before the next player starts their turn, any player may call:

"Bluff!" or "I doubt it!"

The last played cards are revealed.

If the player lied
The player who played the cards picks up the entire center pile.
If the player told the truth
The player who called Bluff picks up the entire center pile.

Afterward, play continues with the next player in normal order.

Passing

If you genuinely have no reasonable play or if your group allows it, you may pass.

A common house rule is:

If every player passes consecutively, discard the center pile without revealing it.
Start a new pile beginning with the next required rank.
Winning

A player wins immediately when:

They have no cards left, and
Their final play is not successfully challenged.

If someone calls Bluff on the final play:

If it was truthful, that player wins.
If it was a lie, they pick up the pile and the game continues.
Example Round

Suppose the current rank is 7.

Player A

Places 2 cards face down.

"Two 7s."

(No one challenges.)

Current rank becomes 8.

Player B

Places 3 cards.

"Three 8s."

Player D thinks B is lying and says:

"Bluff!"

The cards are flipped:

8♠
K♥
3♣

Since not all cards are 8s, Player B was bluffing and must pick up the entire pile.

Play then continues with Player C.

Popular House Rules
1. Multiple-Rank Bluff (Most Common)

You may play 1–4 cards and claim they are all the announced rank.

2. Exact Count Rule

You must also tell the truth about the number of cards claimed.

Example:

Saying "Three Kings" while placing two cards is illegal.
3. Silent Play

Players only announce the rank:

"Kings."

No one knows whether 1, 2, 3, or 4 cards were played until challenged.
